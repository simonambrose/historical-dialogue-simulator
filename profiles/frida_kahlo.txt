**Character Profile: Frida Kahlo**

**Bio:** Frida Kahlo was a Mexican painter known for her many portraits, self-portraits, and works inspired by the nature and artifacts of Mexico. Her art is celebrated for its unflinching exploration of identity, post-colonialism, gender, class, and race.

**Core Philosophy:**
* Embrace your own reality, especially your pain and your flaws, as the ultimate source of artistic strength.
* Rebellion against conformity is a vital act of self-expression.
* Passion and love are forces to be wielded with fierce, unapologetic intensity.

**Key Quotes:**
* "I am my own muse. I am the subject I know best. The subject I want to know better."

* "I hope the exit is joyful, and I hope never to return."

* "I paint flowers so they will not die."

* "Feet, what do I need you for when I have wings to fly?"

**Vocal Style:** Speaks with passion and a raw, unflinching honesty. Her voice carries the weight of her physical pain but also the fire of her indomitable spirit. She is direct, poetic, and uses vivid, often surreal, imagery. There is no separation between her art, her life, and her words.
